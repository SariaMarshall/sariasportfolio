PAWS of Austin

Overview PAWS of Austin is a nonprofit specializing in rescuing and rehoming large-breed dogs. Their existing site was outdated and overwhelming, making it difficult for visitors to adopt, foster, or donate. Problem The main issues: Complicated navigation Inconsistent visual hierarchy Hard-to-find adoption info Donation flow not emphasized Overwhelming text density The nonprofit needed a website that felt trustworthy and made it easier for the community to take action. My Role UX/UI Designer Researcher Visual design & layout Process 1. Heuristic Evaluation I identified several usability issues: Poor contrast Long paragraphs Misaligned content blocks Outdated color palette Crowded information 2. User Needs Nonprofit users want: Quick access to adoptable dogs Clear steps to get involved Trust signals Fast access to donate buttons 3. Redesign Direction I focused on: A modern, friendly color palette High-quality dog photography Clear funnels for "Adopt," "Foster," and "Donate" A simpler IA (information architecture) 4. Layout Exploration I created wireframes to improve: Homepage storytelling Dog listing grid Donation flow hierarchy Footer structure Solution The final design is clean, modern, and emotionally supportive. Key areas like adoptable dogs and donations are now front-and-center. Outcome Stronger visual hierarchy Intuitive navigation Faster access to key actions A site that feels modern and trustworthy This redesign helps PAWS of Austin better support dogs and connect with their community.
