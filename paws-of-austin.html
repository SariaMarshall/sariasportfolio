PAWS of Austin

Role: UX/UI Designer

Responsibilities: UX research, user flows, wireframing, prototyping, and UI design end-to-end.

Redesigning a nonprofit dog rescue website for clarity, trust, and accessibility.

Problem

Pet owners struggle to find trustworthy, available sitters without spending excessive time comparing profiles and managing communication across platforms.

Solution

I designed a streamlined booking flow that emphasizes sitter trust indicators, availability clarity, and simple communication tools.

Outcome

The concept delivers a more confident, efficient booking experience that prioritizes pet safety and user peace of mind.

Project Overview

PAWS of Austin is a nonprofit focused on rescuing and rehoming large-breed dogs. Their website was outdated, disorganized, and did not effectively guide users to adopt, volunteer, or donate. My redesign focused on clarity, emotion, and improving the overall user experience.

Problem

  • Navigation was unclear and pages were difficult to find.
  • No clear call-to-action for adopting or donating.
  • Visual design was outdated and not mobile-friendly.

Goals

  • Modernize the UI with bright, uplifting visuals.
  • Make adoption flows clearer and easier.
  • Highlight success stories and available dogs.
  • Encourage donations with accessible CTAs.
